Animals

Pink Floyd
Released January 23, 1977

Pink Floyd’s tenth studio album, Animals was released in early 1977. The band’s previous release Wish You Were Here was among the best-selling albums of 1975. Also, Pink Floyd received critical acclaim for it, getting five stars in some of the most popular music magazines of the seventies. After that, Roger Waters decided to hoard all lyrical works for the next album, Animals. The entire theme of the album is highly influenced by George Orwell’s 1945 novella, Animal Farm. He stated that he wanted to do some kind of “tribute” to George Orwell’s masterpiece (Animal Farm). David Gilmour, in an interview with Mojo, talked about the idea that Waters had to become the main (and sometimes only) writer of the band’s songs.
